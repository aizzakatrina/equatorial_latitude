Humidity

A majority of cities near the equator have high humidity levels, although there are several cities near the equator that do not. We would expect humidity levels to increase as we move closer to the equator, but the data displays an inconsistent pattern. Several cities further away from the equator also have high humidity levels. Since humidity can indicate the likelihood of precipitation, dew, or fog, it is likely that the the high humidity levels north of the equator are related to rain and fog which is consistent with the season at that time of the year.
